## Q3 — Anything strange or likely not what you actually wanted?

1. **The "Gaps" subsections contradict their own Status lines.** Reading the roadmap top-down, B9.3 says `[DONE 2026-06-01]` and then lists six `[NOT STARTED]` gaps that are in fact implemented (see Q2). Same for B9.2/B9.4. **Recommendation:** prune or re-mark the Gaps lists so the doc is internally consistent; right now it can't be trusted as a to-do source without re-verifying each line against code.

2. **Internal tracker contradictions.** B9.7 is `[NOT STARTED]` in its header but `✅` in the tracker. B9.2's gap says "tests don't exist," the tracker says `✅ (59)`. B9.3's gap says "tests don't exist," the status says "31 tests pass." These are the kind of self-contradiction that makes the status markers unreliable.

3. **An entire dialect/utility subsystem exists in code but is undocumented in any plan.** Not in the roadmap or B9 plans: `validate_dialect.py`, `find_missing_dialect.py`, `_dialect_inventory.py`, `_corpus_index.py` (+ `test_corpus_index.py`, 15 tests), `fix_dialects.py`, `fix_ids.py` (+ `test_fix_ids.py`, 8 tests), `fix_multiple_translations.py`. Most appear to have landed under the catch-all commit `87cdc6aac "A whole bunch of updates to QC."` `validate_dialect.py` is a sensible companion to V036, but **none of this is tracked against intent** — worth deciding where it belongs in the plan (likely B9.6) and documenting it.

4. **Highest untested-risk surface is `corpus_metrics.py` (581 statements, 0% coverage) — and it feeds a CI workflow** (`corpus-metrics.yaml`, which auto-commits `statistics/`). `count_tokens.py` (feeds `token-comparison.yaml`) is also 0%. Both were claimed tested (Q1). The two CI-coupled metrics scripts are the least-tested code in the repo. If you wanted "highest-risk QC code" tested first (A's stated goal), these arguably qualify and were skipped.

5. **`validate_orthography.py` and `validate_vocabulary.py` have zero tests** (absent from the coverage report → never imported by a test). Consistent with B9.6 "partial (extract only)," but flagging because they're the B9.6 workhorses and are entirely unexercised.

6. **The xfail mechanism the roadmap leans on is now fully historical.** The doc describes "19 xfails that flip to passing as B lands"; there are now **0 xfailed** tests. That's the *good* outcome, but it means the A-status line and several mentions of xfail counts are stale narration.

7. **`gloss.py` has two rules beyond what any plan documents** (`v066_clitic_in_W_requires_clitic_in_M`, `v067_no_angle_brackets_in_M_FORM`). Not a problem — but they're undocumented invariants now gating data, which is the same "code ahead of docs" pattern as #3.

- **Q3:** Main concerns are (a) the roadmap's Gaps subsections are internally inconsistent and over-report remaining work, (b) an undocumented dialect/fix-utility subsystem landed outside the plan, and (c) the two CI-coupled metrics scripts are the most-trusted-yet-least-tested code in the repo.
