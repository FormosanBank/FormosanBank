Formosan Languages
臺灣南島語
kakaiyan nua senantaw i Taiwan

The Formosan languages belong to the Austronesian language family, one of the world's largest and most geographically widespread language groups. While the Austronesian languages extend from Madagascar in the west to Easter Island in the east, Taiwan is home to a significant number of unique languages within this family. These languages are collectively known as the Formosan languages.
臺灣南島語屬於南島語系，其 為世界上最大且地理分布最廣 的語系之一。南島語系的分布 範圍從西邊的馬達加斯加一直 到東邊的復活節島，而臺灣正 是此語系中許多獨特語言的發 源地，這些語言被統稱為臺灣 南島語。

Origin and Historical Background
起源與歷史背景
sangasan a likisi katua cinalivatan

The Austronesian-speaking people are believed to have first settled in Taiwan around 6,000 years ago, making it one of the earliest regions to develop distinct Austronesian languages.
據信，南島語族約在 6000 年前 首次定居臺灣，使臺灣成為最 早發展出獨特南島語的地區之 一。
seladenga itjen tu aza nantaw zuku patagilj a kaimaza i Taiwan izuanga 6,000 a cavilj, avansika sipazurung tua namakakaizuazua sa maretimalji a kemacalisian imaza.

The diversity among the Formosan languages is a testament to this long history, as the island served as a hub of linguistic evolution and cultural exchange before the Austronesian people began migrating to other parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.
在南島民族遷徙至東南亞 及太平洋其他地區之前，臺灣 一直是語言演化與文化交流的 中心，而臺灣南島語的多樣性 正見證了這段悠久的歷史。

The Formosan Languages Today
臺灣南島語現狀
situcuan a kemacalisian

Today, there are 16 officially recognized Formosan languages, though their vitality varies greatly. Formosan languages today are spoken only by a portion of Taiwan's indigenous population, constituting ~2% of Taiwan's population. Therefore, many of these languages are considered endangered, with only a few actively spoken in daily life. The speakers often reside in specific Indigenous communities, and revitalization efforts are ongoing to ensure these languages survive for future generations.
如今，臺灣共有 16 種官方認定 的臺灣南島語言，但其生命力 差異甚鉅。目前，臺灣南島語 僅由臺灣原住民族一部分人口 使用，這些原住民族約佔臺灣 總人口的 2%。因此，許多臺灣 南島語被認為正處於瀕危狀 態，只有少數幾種語言仍在日 常生活當中被使用。而這些使 用者主要分布於特定的原住民 族地區(部落)。為此，復振 語言的工作正持續進行，期以 確保這些語言在未來世代中能 繼續保存。

A Legacy of Suppression and Revitalization
受壓抑與復振的語言
napaselapai sa papilazek a kakaiyan

Throughout history, the use of Formosan languages has been suppressed under different colonial regimes. 
歷史上，臺灣南島語的使用在 不同殖民政權下遭到壓制。
itua cinalivatan a likisi, aza kai nua kacalisian, ayatua maljian a paljavak a mareka sihu.

he most striking example occurred during the Mandarin-only policy from 1949 to 1987, which enforced Mandarin Chinese as the sole official language, significantly impacting the transmission of Formosan languages to younger generations.
最 具代表性的例子發生於 1949 年至 1987 年間的單一國語政 策，該政策將國語(華語)強 制為唯一的官方語言，對臺灣 南島語的傳承，尤其是對年輕 一代的傳遞，造成無比深遠的 影響。
tiamadju nasema picul a lemivu katua maselapai, parutua aicu a pinenetjan pinaka amin a ita a kai a ciniukukuan kemasi 1949 patje 1987 a caviljan. avansika dikinay anga macekaw tua aicu a kemacalisian tazua. sika tjadjaljaw a maqulip anga za kai, utauta namasan pazangal tu pazurung ta kai itua maqacuvucuvung. 

In recent years, there has been a resurgence of interest in revitalizing these languages, driven by government initiatives and grassroots efforts.
近年來，復振這些語言的興趣 逐漸重燃，這一趨勢主要得益 於政府倡議和草根運動的努 力。

This includes language education in schools, proficiency exams, immersion programs, and media broadcasting in Indigenous languages.
這些復振工作包括學校中 的語言教育、語言能力認證測 驗、沉浸式教學課程，以及原 住民族語言的媒體廣播。
avansika izua mareka sengsengan pakatua kai a paljazua pitua sinupuan i kakituluan, a pitua sisekingan, a pitua tilibi katua seman kamukuan.

The Indigenous Languages Research and Development Foundation and various local organizations play a vital role in these efforts.
