### Notes - Ortho113 Orthography:
- The letter 'l' represents [ɗ], a voiced alveolar implosive
- 'h' represents [h], a glottal fricative (different from MinEd)
- 'r' represents [ɽ], a retroflex tap
- 'ng' represents the velar nasal [ŋ]
- Glottal stop is represented by apostrophe (')
- 'w' consonant appears to be missing from this orthography

## Key Differences Between Orthographies

| Letter | MinEd IPA | Ortho113 IPA | Difference |
|--------|-----------|--------------|------------|
| l      | [d]       | [ɗ]          | Plain voiced stop vs. implosive |
| h      | [x]       | [h]          | Velar fricative vs. glottal fricative |
| r      | [r]       | [ɽ]          | Trill vs. retroflex tap |
| w      | [w]       | (absent)     | Present in MinEd, absent in Ortho113 |

### Consonant Inventory Summary

**MinEd Orthography:** 18 consonants  
**Ortho113 Orthography:** 17 consonants (missing 'w')

Both orthographies include:
- Implosive consonants (b, l)
- Voiceless affricate (c)
- Full series of fricatives (f, v, s, z, h)
- Three nasals (m, n, ng)
- Liquid consonant (r)
- Glottal stop (')
- Palatal glide (y)

The main differences lie in the phonetic realization of certain letters rather than the overall phonological structure.
